Good questions

Everything you might be wondering

How does the SOS button work?
Hold the red SOS button for three seconds to dispatch emergency services. The hold prevents accidental triggers, and you can release any time to cancel. Once dispatched, your live location, vitals, allergies, conditions and emergency contacts are sent to the responder automatically, and you see live ETA and a direct line to the dispatcher.
Is my health data really private?
Yes. Your vault is end-to-end encrypted on your device and locked to your phone — verified once with a one-time code. Not even we can read your records without your key. EaseMyDisease is HIPAA-compliant, and you can export everything (FHIR or PDF) or delete your account at any time.
What does “time-boxed sharing” mean?
When you share a record with a doctor, you choose exactly what they see and for how long — 24 hours, 7 days or 30 days. Access auto-expires, and you can revoke it instantly. An access log shows you everyone who has ever viewed your records.
How does scanning records actually work?
Snap a lab report or prescription with your camera, upload a PDF/photo, connect a provider like Quest or LabCorp, or sync Apple Health. Our OCR reads the document, extracts the values, files it under the right category, and links it to the relevant organ on your body map.
Can I manage my family’s health too?
Absolutely. The Family plan gives one account up to six vaults — for your partner, parents and kids. You can manage their records, medications and emergencies, see each person’s health score, and switch profiles in a tap.
Which languages are supported?
The app is available in English, Spanish and Hindi, and in-consultation live translation works across all three — so you can speak with a doctor in your language even if they speak another.
